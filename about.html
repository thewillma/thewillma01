THE WILLMA YOUTH CLUB

Preamble

The WillMa youth club is an integral part of a community give back project. The purpose of the project is to serve, promote youth involvement and provide assistance, safe and accessible space for young people to meet and socialise for personal and community development. This project is intended to inspire and motivate young people to have confidence and be more active in their personal and community engagement. We also aim at building a unified network of young people who will act as volunteers in serving their communities and champion sustainable development through civic engagement.

The Birth of Willma Youth Club; The journey of Josephine; Founder/President

The club emerged from the frequent association of the founder of the Club with young people and the desire to empower young people from the rural communities. The idea to serve her community through youth empowerment and engagement was birthed since she gained the MasterCard Foundation Scholarship under CAMFED in 2014 and went for the scholars' leadership and enrichment camp 2014 and met other young great minds who inspired her with their exceptional experience in education and social live. After she completed SHS, she had the opportunity to serve as a learner guide in Lassie Tuolu SHS. Engaging with the students made her realized a capability of changing mindsets and empowering others who are less confident to be more confident. She moved on to facilitate at scholars' camp 2017 and 2018, she drives inspirations from her daily activities with youth and envisioned the impact she could make bringing young people together for a common purpose. Combining passion with family and community engagement, this club finally came to public after the founder decided to keep in touch with her mentees as a learner facilitator at the St. Francis of Assisi Girl S.H.S in 2021.
